Most Played Games of All Time

What this is

Each year I publish a ranking of the board games that have been played the most on BoardGameGeek (BGG) since January 2010. The list complements the rankings you can find on BGG itself, which are based on ratings — i.e. on how much people like games. Here, the question is different: which games actually get played, repeatedly, by many people?

The data

BGG publishes a monthly list of the games most played by its community, where “most played” is measured by the number of distinct players who logged at least one play of the game that month. Since 2010, I've collected the top 100 entries from each month.

How the points work

For each month a game appears in BGG's monthly top 100, it earns points based on its position. The formula is:

points(rank) = rank−0.5

That is: a game ranked #1 earns 1 point, a game ranked #4 earns 0.5, #100 earns 0.1, and so on. The total score for a game is the sum of those monthly points across every month it has been in the top 100.

This formula rewards both consistency (a game that stays high for many years accumulates a lot) and peaks (a single month at #1 is worth more than several months at #50). The diminishing-returns shape (an inverse square root) means there's a meaningful gap between the top of the monthly list and the lower positions, but not an extreme one.

The fields on each game

Some terms used on game cards may need clarification:

Rank
Position in this list, computed from the points formula above.
Points
The total score described above, summed over all monthly top-100 appearances.
Months in BGG's monthly top 100 / top 10
How many distinct months the game was in BGG's monthly top 100 (or top 10) by number of distinct players.
Peak
The best position ever reached in BGG's monthly top by distinct players.
Currently in BGG's monthly top 100
The position the game holds in the most recent month covered by the edition. Shown only for games that are still active.
Out of BGG's monthly top 100 for N months
How long the game has been absent from the monthly top 100, counting from the most recent month covered by the edition.
↑N / ↓N / new / =
Change in rank since last year's edition. ↑N = climbed N places, ↓N = dropped, = = same rank, new = wasn't in last year's list.
Players (official) / (community)
Official: number of players as printed on the box. Community: the player counts BGG users say work in practice (which can be tighter, or include solo when not officially supported).
Best
The player count(s) BGG users find optimal.
Age (official) / (community)
Official: minimum age recommended by the publisher. Community: recommended by BGG users.
Complexity (BGG weight)
A 1-to-5 community measure of how complex the game is to learn and play. 1 = light, 5 = heavy. See the BGG wiki for details.

Editions

Each edition is published every May, covering data up to and including April of that year. So the current (…) edition uses data from January 2010 through April ….
